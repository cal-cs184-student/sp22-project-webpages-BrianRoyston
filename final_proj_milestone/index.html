This is the milestone report for the project "Dynamic Diuse Global Illumination using Precomputed Light Field Probes". By extending
the the idea of irradiance volumes in conventional light eld probe based solutions, we aim to provide a solution for real-time indirect
diuse lighting. As the milestone for rst three weeks, we have completed oine calculation and implemented the irradiance update
at runtime stage. We are one step away to nalize the pipeline and expecting nishing as plan in time.

2.2 Runtime Processing
For the runtime stage, we have implemented the update of radiance and reconstruction of irradiance. The whole update
process for both radiance and irradiance is performed on the gpu using the compute shaders.
At the beginning of each frame, each pixel of the probes are re-lighted utilizing the pre-calculated information from
GBuer, including its albedo color, normal and skybox visibility. The contribution from indirect diuse and skybox
color will be linear interpolated with fraction of sky visibility. In order to achieve a proper balance between the skybox
and the indirect diuse, we implemented the feature to adjust the intensity of the skybox.

3 PLAN
To nalize the runtime rendering pipeline, we will sample the nearest 8 probes given a surface’s world position and
normal with proper clamping on the grid’s edges. We will weight the 8 probes based on 3 criterias: the trilinear
interpolation based on probes’ positions, the dot product between the direction to the probe and the sample normal and
the visibility test based on PCF occlusion test or Chebychev occlusion test. Beside, as an optional alternate, we will try
sampling the indirect lighting of the probes from the results of the previous frame during the radiance update, which
will give us innite light bounces in an ideal situation.
Our current progress follows well to the original timeline from proposal, and we plan to continue following it to the
submission. The nalization of completed pipeline could be expected by the end of April and the deliverables including
nal paper will be organized by the rst week of May.
